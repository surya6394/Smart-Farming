Arhar

Among the pulse crops, tur is the place after gram in our state, this crop is sown alone and also with other crops. Jowar, bajra, urd and cotton are the major crops sown along with tur. The productivity of our state is more than the all India average. This can be further increased by adopting intensive methods.

Farm selection:-

Sandy loam and loamy land is good for arhar crop. Proper drainage and lightly sloping fields are best for tur. It cannot be done successfully in saline and alkaline soil.

Field Preparation:-

After doing the first plowing of the field with soil turning plow, 2-3 ploughings should be done with indigenous plow. After plowing, the field should be prepared by planting a pad.

Time of sowing:-

Late maturing varieties which mature in about 270 days should be sown in the month of July. Early maturing varieties should be sown in irrigated areas by the middle of June, so that this crop is ready by the end of November and sowing of wheat is possible in the first fortnight of December. In order to get higher yield, emphasis has been given in zayed to sow Ta-21 species in the first fortnight of April (except the lowlands in the plains of the state) as a co-crop with summer moong. it has two benefits.

  • The crop is ready by mid-November and there is no delay in sowing of wheat.
  • Its yield is higher than the crop sown in June (kharif).
  • Sowing on bunds gives good yield.

Seed treatment:-

First one kg. Treat the seed with 2 gm Thiram, 1 gm Carbondazim or 4 gm Trichoderma + 1 gm Carboxin. Before sowing, treat each seed with a specific rhizobium culture of tur. One packet is 10 kg. Sufficient for seed. Dissolve one packet Rhizobium culture in clean water and mix it with 10 kg. Sprinkle it over the seed and mix it with a light hand so that a light layer is formed on the seed. Sow this seed immediately. There is a possibility of the culture bacteria dying from strong sunlight. In such fields where tur is being sown for the first time after a long time, the culture must be used.

Seed quantity and sowing method:-

Sowing should be done in the litter behind the plow. According to the species and season, the amount of seed and the distance of sowing should be kept as follows. After 20-25 days of sowing, the distance of the plant should be fixed by removing the dense plant. If sowing is done by ridge method, the yield is more. In case of delay in sowing due to floods or incessant rains in eastern Uttar Pradesh, sowing can be done in the first fortnight of september as a spring crop, but the row-to-row distance is 30 cm. And the quantity of seed is 20-25 kg/ha. rate should be used.

Application of Fertilizers:-

Di ammonium phosphate and 20 kg. Sulfur should be given in rows at the time of sowing with the help of a robe or barber so that the fertilizer does not contact with the seed. It would be appropriate that the entire quantity of phosphorus should be given from single super phosphate so that 12 percent sulfur can also be supplied. Small quantity of urea manure (15-20 kg per hectare) should be given only in those fields which are weak in nitrogen content. Give half the amount of manure used for tur to co-crops like urd. At the time of sowing as co-crops of jowar and bajra, 20 kg in their rows. Nitrogen per ha. and after one month 10 kg. Topdressing of nitrogen per hectare should be done in 30 to 40 kg for sowing in September. Good yield is obtained by using nitrogen per hectare.

Irrigation:-

Arhar Ta-21 and UPAS-120 and ICPL-151 should be sown and other species should be sown when there is sufficient moisture in the rainy season. In case of low moisture in the field, one irrigation must be done in the month of October at the time of legume formation. Irrigation in the month of December or January is beneficial to avoid frost in late maturing species.

weeding:-

During the rainy season, the growth of weeds becomes sufficient after about three to four weeks. Therefore, one weeding should be done within one month of sowing. If tur has been cultivated in pure form, then it will be necessary to do the second weeding 20 days after the first. To destroy weeds and broad-leaf weeds by chemical method, dissolve 3.3 liters of Pendamethalin (30 EC) or 4 liters of Elaclor (50 EC) in 700-800 liters of water immediately after sowing. Spray before or after dissolving Fluchloralin (45 EC) 2.2 liters in required water and mix it in the soil by spraying the land before the last ploughing.
