Cotton:-

Maharashtra reported an all-time high 44 lakh hectares of cotton acreage and production of 407 lakh quintals (80 lakh bales each of 170 kg) of cotton. Since the start of the season, the fibre crop has been trading well below its government declared minimum support price (MSP) of Rs 5,500 per quintal. Both the Cotton Corporation of India (CCI) and the Maharashtra State Cooperative Cotton Marketing Federation had waded into procurement of cotton to help farmers. Till March 2020 — that is before the lockdown was announced — both the CCI and the federation had effected payment of 145.06 lakh quintals of cotton.

Crop Production Techniques:

Climate Requirement:

It is necessary to have a minimum temperature of 18 degree centigrade for best deposit, 21 to 27 degree centigrade temperature at the time of crop growth and 27 to 32 degree centigrade temperature during the day and coolness at night for proper fruiting. Bright sunshine and frost free season is necessary for the sycamores to burst.

Earth:-

Soily, alkaline, pebbled and waterlogged lands are unsuitable for cotton. Cotton can be cultivated successfully in all other lands.

Field preparation:

A deep plowing (20-25 cm) should be done by first plowing the soil with a soil turning plow. Secondly, the field should be prepared by doing three to four ploughings with cultivator or country plow. For best germination, the soil must be well-rotted. In districts like Mathura, Agra, where there is saline water in the tube wells, prepare the field by feeding it with water from the canals.

The process of burning the fibers takes 2-3 minutes. As soon as the fibers burn. Add 10 liters of clean water and stir the seeds with a stick or stick. After this, pour the acid solution containing the seeds through a plastic mesh with fine holes, drain it completely and wash it with clean water three to four times. Dip the clean washed seed in the chemical solution (50 g sodium bicarbonate and 10 liters of water) for 1 minute and wash the seed once again with clean water (by doing this the residual acid will be removed).

    Pruning:-

    Due to excessive and untimely rainfall, the height of plants usually exceeds 1.5 meters, which adversely affects the yield. Therefore, all the branches above the main stem having a height of more than 1.5 meters should be trimmed/sketched. This sorting makes it easy to spray insecticidal chemicals and the use of spraying is completely possible.

    weed control-

    To get good yield of cotton, complete weed control is very important. For this, weeding should be done three-four times by tractor driven cultivator or bullock driven Tripali cultivator at the time of crop growth. The first weeding should be dry, which should be done before the first irrigation (30-35 days before sowing). Cultivators should not be used when flowers and sycamores are formed. In these conditions, weeds should be removed by self..

    Irrigation and Drainage:-

    Irrigation and drainage have a significant impact on cotton yield. First irrigation should be done 30-35 days after sowing. If there is no rain, irrigation is required at an interval of 2-3 weeks. Take special care that there should never be a shortage of water while making flowers and sycamores, otherwise there will be excessive shedding of buds, flowers and sycamores.
