Sorghum:-

It is being cultivated in Maharashtra both for grain and fodder during kharif (area 13.84 lakh ha) and rabi (area 30.17 lakh ha). Kharif sorghum is also becoming popular as poultry and animal feed. The percentage area under rabi sorghum (64 %) is more than kharif sorghum (36 %) area. Contrary to this, production and average yields of kharif sorghum are higher (more than 1 tonnes/ha) than the average yield in rabi (0.6 tonne/ha). In kharif cultivation, the major constraints are lower profit and non-competitiveness than cotton, sunflower, castor and pulses; grain mould susceptibility during the years of extended monsoon at grain maturity and susceptibility to stem-borer under dry weather condition, and inadequate availability of seeds of improved dual-purpose varieties and hybrids of sorghum in time at fair price.

Field selection and preparation::-

The sandy loam or such land with good drainage system is suitable for the cultivation of jowar. In Bundelkhand region, jowar is usually cultivated in medium heavy and sloping land. The first ploughing with soil-reversing plow and the other two or three ploughings with indigenous plow should be done to prepare the field well. soluble complete or thiophanate methyl at the rate of 1.5 grams per seed. Before sowing, treat the seed with Rhizobium specific to soybean. One packet is 10 kg. Sufficient for seed. 10 kg of culture in one packet. Sprinkle it over the seed and mix it with a light hand so that a light layer is formed on the seed. Sow this seed immediately. There is a possibility of killing the bacteria of the culture due to strong sunlight, in such fields where soybean is being sown for the first time or after a long time, the culture must be used.

Sowing:

Time

More time for sowing of jowar from the last week of June to the first week of July is applicable.

Seed Rate

1 ha. For field sowing 10-12 kg seed is required.

  • Hybrid 7-8 kg/ha..
  • Packages 10-12 kg/ha.

Bozo treatment:-

Before sowing, one kilogram of seed should be treated with 2.5 gm of Thiram, which gives good deposition and does not cause tubercle disease. To avoid the infestation of termites, treat with chlorpyrifos at the rate of 25 ml per kg of seed..

Distance of rows and plants:-

Sowing of jowar is 45 cm. should be done at a distance of behind the plow. Plant to plant distance is 15-20 cm. Should be Indigenous jowar is mixed with tur and sown by the method of sprinkling. The yield is not good in this. Therefore, sowing one row of jowar between two rows of late ripening tur would be appropriate.

Fertilizers::-

It would be preferable to use fertilizers on the basis of soil test. For best yield 80:40:20 kg for hybrid species and 40:20:20 kg for other species Phosphorus and potash should be used per hectare. Half of the amount of nitrogen and the full amount of phosphorus and potash should be put under the seeds in the funds at the time of sowing in the field and the remaining 1/2 part of nitrogen should be used in the standing crop after about 30-35 days of sowing.

Irrigation:-

Irrigation must be done if there is less moisture in the field at the time of earing and grain filling. Otherwise it has an adverse effect on the yield.

Weeding hoeing::-

After three weeks of sowing, weeding should be done. If the number of plants in the field is more, then the distance should be fixed by thinning. For chemical control, spray atrazine 50% @ 1.5 kg per 2 days after dissolving in 800 liters of water. Weed control should be done by ploughing the crops sown in rows.

Crop Protection:

Pests:

Sorghum shoot fly identification It is smaller in size than the house fly whose young (magget) damages the crop as soon as the deposit starts.

Treatment:

Qunalfas 25 EC Spray 1.5 liters per hectare.

Stem borer pest:-

Identification:

The larva of this insect keeps eating inside by piercing the stem, due to which the cabbage of the seed dries up.

Treatment:

Use the remedies mentioned for the stem borer of maize.

Ear Head Midge::-

Identification:

The adult midge is red in color and lays eggs on the flower leaf. The red maggots stay inside the grains and suck its juice, due to which the grains dry up. the

Treatment:

Carbaryl (50 percent soluble powder) 1.25 kg/ha.

Ear Head Cater Pillar:-

Identification:

Its girders eat soft grains and destroy them and make a web in the cob.

Treatment:

Qunalfas 25 EC Spray 1.5 liters per hectare.

Tidal Mite:-

Identification:

It is a very small octagonal, which by weaving webs on the lower surface of the leaves and staying inside them, sucks the juice from the leaves. The affected leaves turn red in color and dry up.

Treatment:

One of the following chemicals should be sprayed. Dimethoate (30 per hectare or Chlorpyriphos 25 EC 1.5-2.00 l/ha..
