Scientific Cultivation of Hybrid Paddy:-

Uttar Pradesh is the largest state with maximum contribution towards national production (35.03%) from a large area (35.12%), but with productivity on a lower side of 2.7 tonnes/ha. The wheat production is distributed in three agroclimatic zones, viz. western Uttar Pradesh (3.29 million ha), eastern Uttar Pradesh (5.24 million ha) and central Uttar Pradesh (0.68 million ha). The yield gap between farmers fields and frontline demonstration is 1.35 tonnes/ha. The area is 9.2 million ha, with a production of 24.5 million tonnes and productivity of 2.7 tonnes/ha. The trend during the last five years has shown a marginal decline in production and productivity from nearly stable area of cultivation. The major constraints in production are decreased soil organic carbon status, nutrient mining, imbalanced fertilization, crop residue burning leading to nutrient and organic carbon loss, declining watertable and late sowing under sugarcane-wheat and after potato in western Uttar Pradesh

In order to get high yields, it is necessary to pay attention to the following things:

  • Sowing of pure and certified seeds should be done after seed treatment.
  • The species should be selected according to regional adaptability and time-specific.
  • Seeds must be changed in the third year.
  • Fertilizers should be used in the right way at the right time on the basis of soil test in balanced quantity.
  • Irrigation at critical stages (Tajmool stage and Pushya stage) should be done in proper method and quantity from time to time.
  • Special attention should be paid to the prevention of insects and diseases.
  • When there is an outbreak of wheat, it should be controlled in time.
  • Other activities should be completed on time based on the recommendation.
  • Use zero tillage and raised weed method..
  • Rotavator harrow should be used for field preparation..
  • Bio-fertilizers should be used..
  • Half the amount of manure should be done with bio-wash manures as far as possible..

Smooth Methods:-

In case of irrigated sowing:-

About 97 percent of the total wheat area in the state is irrigated but in some areas assured or assured Irrigation is available. Therefore, understanding how we can increase the yield of wheat with limited means of irrigation and fertilizers, it should be used by selecting the progressive species.

Preparation of the field :

Wheat is mostly sown after paddy. Therefore, the sowing of wheat is often delayed. Us It has to be decided in advance that which variety of paddy to choose in Kharif and its varieties in Rabi. Which variety of wheat to sow after? Timely transplanting of paddy is necessary to get good yield of wheat.

Nursery Management:

The nursery management of hybrid rice is different from that of other high yielding common varieties. For planting hybrid paddy in one hectare area, nursery of 700 to 800 square meter area is sufficient, which is also desired for normal paddy. Keep in mind that even though the quantity of hybrid paddy seed is less for nursery, it is not appropriate to reduce the area. As a result, the plants in the nursery are rare and they grow well. 100 kg before nursery sowing. Nitrogen, 50 kg phosphorus and 50 kg. Potash is applied in the field at the rate of per hectare. Spraying of 0.5 percent zinc sulphate and 0.2 percent ferrous sulphate solution is desired if symptoms of zinc or iron deficiency are seen in the nursery. So that the fields for wheat become vacant in the month of October, the second thing to be noted is that the land becomes hard due to pelting/service in paddy. In heavy land, it would be appropriate to sow wheat by first plowing the soil after July with disc harrow and making the soil friable. With the help of that the paddy is cut into small pieces. They must be given 15-20 Kia Nitrogen (in the form of urea) per hectare on the first plowing while preparing the field for early sunning. The field is completely ready in a single plowing by tractor driven rotavator.

sowing:-

Wheat should be sown on time and at sufficient moisture. Late maturing varieties must be sown on time. Otherwise, there is a reduction in yield. As sowing is delayed, the rate of decline in wheat yield increases. When sowing in December, the yield of wheat decreases at the rate of 3 to 4 q/0 and on sowing in January, at the rate of 4 to 5 q/0 per week, sowing of wheat should be done with seed.

Seed Rate and Seed Treatment:-

In line sowing, in normal condition 100 kg and coarse grain is 125 kg and in case of sprinkling sowing normal grain is 125 kg and coarse grain is 150 kg per, should be used. Be sure to check the deposition percentage before sowing. This facility is available free of cost at government research centers. If the seed germination capacity is less, then increase the seed rate accordingly and if the seed is not certified then it must be treated. Sow the seeds after treating them with Carboxyl Agentbacter and PSV. In areas with limited irrigation, use Razed Weed method at the rate of 75 kg and coarse grain at the rate of 100 kg per hectare.

Row spacing:

The spacing of rows is 18 cm to 23 cm in normal condition. and depth 5cm.

In case of delayed sowing:

15 cm to 18 cm and depth 4 cm in case of late sowing.

Method:-

Sowing should be done in the huts behind the plough or by Ferti seed drill on the proper moisture content of the soil. It is preferable to sow only after harvesting. It should be noted that there must be 400 to 500 spiked plants per square meter after flowering, otherwise it will have adverse effect on the yield. To avoid delay, sow with Pantnagar Zerotrail seed and fertilizer drill. Sowing by tractor operated roto till drill is more profitable. Sowing should be done in Bundelkhand (Bhar and Kawar soil) without plowing so that the deposition is correct.

irrigation:-

It is necessary to irrigate the field two days after transplanting. Keep in mind that it should not remain constant in the land and it is beneficial to maintain the water level in the field for 5 patients at the stage of filling the grain. it happens.

Application of Fertilizers:-

Quantity

It is advisable to use fertilizers on the basis of soil test. For good yield of dwarf wheat. After kharif crops of maize, paddy, jowar, bajra, nitrogen, phosphorus and potash should be used at the rate of 150:60:40 kg per hectare and late at 80:40:30 respectively. In normal condition in Bundelkhand region 120:60:40 kg. Nitrogen, Phosphorus and Potash and 30 kg. The use of sulfur per hectare has been found beneficial. In areas where DAP is being used continuously, 30 kg. Use of sulfur will be beneficial. If the field has been fallow in Kharif or pulses have been sown, then the amount of nitrogen is 20 kg. Use less per hectare.

Time and Method:

To increase the fertilizer efficiency, they should be used in different types of land in the following way.

Loam or Matiyar, Kawar and Mar:

Half of Nitrogen, Phosphate and Potash should be given 2-3 cm below the seed in the pit at the time of sowing. Give the remaining amount of nitrogen 24 hours before the first irrigation or after the onset of oats. 2- 1/3 quantity of nitrogen, phosphate and potash should be given under the seeds in the litter at the time of sowing in sandy loam, hard and padwa sandy soil. Half of the remaining nitrogen should be given after the first irrigation (20-25 days) (crown root stage) and the remaining quantity after the second irrigation. In such soils it is more beneficial to do top dressing only after irrigation. One who is capable of giving only 40 kg of nitrogen and two irrigations, he should place all the nitrogen at the time of sowing in heavy loamy land, but where there is light loamy land, use half of the nitrogen at the time of sowing (placement) and the rest for the first time. Topdressing should be done on irrigation.

In case of assured irrigation:-

Generally, in order to get maximum yield of dwarf wheat, irrigation should be done in light soil at the following stages. The lack of water at these stages has a severe effect on the yield, but do light irrigation.

First Irrigation:

20-25 days after crown root sowing (Tajmool stage)

Second Irrigation

40-45 days after sowing (at the time of emergence of buds)

Third Irrigation:

60-65 days after sowing (at the time of formation of long knots or bales)

Fourth Irrigation:

At 80-85 days after sowing (paulage)

Fifth Irrigation:

at 100-105 days after sowing (milk stage)

Sixth Irrigation:

On 115-120 days of sowing (at the time of seed filling), good yield can be obtained by doing the following four irrigations in loamy or heavy loamy land, but Make each irrigation a few deep (8 cm).

  • 20-25 days after sowing the first irrigation
  • Second irrigation 30 days after the first.
  • Third irrigation 30 days after the second.
  • Fourth irrigation 20-25 days after third

In case of limited irrigation facilities::

If only three irrigation facilities are available, then do the Taj Mool stage before the earing and at the milk stage. If only two irrigations are available then do it at the crown root and flowering stage, if only one irrigation is available then do it at the crown root stage.

In the irrigation of wheat, pay attention to the following 3

  • Before sowing, level the field well and give a slight slope in one direction, so that To have a uniform distribution across the farm.
  • sowing, according to the soil and the means of irrigation, divide the field into beds of the required size tirelessly. This helps in uniform distribution of water.
  • If there is assured irrigation facility in light lands, irrigation is light (about 6 cm of water) and loam and soil. Irrigate a little deep(about 8 cm of water per irrigation) in the soil and in the case of irrigation equipment. Note: The first irrigation should done 28-30 days after sowing and the remaining irrigations should be done light and early. So that the soil does not dry out.

Harvesting:

The crop should be harvested immediately when the earrings are ripe (physical maturity), otherwise there is a possibility of grain loss. In the case of bad weather, use of combined harvester is preferable, which can avoid losses.

Special Points:

Store the grains in metal buckets or closets or rooms as per convenience. By the way, the metal-made bakhari is very suitable for storage. Before storage, clean the rooms and rooms and sprinkle Malathion 50 percent solution (1 100) on the walls and floor at the rate of 3 liters per 100 sqm. Apply polythene on the lid of Bukhari and coat it with soil so that it becomes airtight.

In case of irrigated and delayed sowing:-

Wheat is sown after early paddy, rapeseed, potato, sugarcane paddy and early ripening tur.

  • For late sowing, select species according to regional adaptability, which have been described earlier.
  • In case of delay sowing should be done with zero tillage machine..
  • Seed rate of 125 kg per hectare and fertilizer (80 40 30) must be used in balanced quantity.
  • After soaking the seeds in water overnight, keep it for 24 hours and sow it on proper soil moisture.
  • In late wheat, irrigation is required more often than usual. First irrigation should be done after 15-20 days of set-up and top-tracing should be done. Subsequent irrigation should be done at an interval of 15-20 days.
