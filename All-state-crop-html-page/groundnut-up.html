Groundnut

Groundnut is the main oilseed crop of Kharif. It protects the land from being cut by wind and rain. In peanut grains, 22-28 percent protein, 10-12 percent carbohydrate, 48-50 percent fat is found. 100 cm. Groundnut yields are good in areas with annual rainfall. It is mainly grown in large area in Jhansi, Hardoi, Sitapur Kheri, Unnao, Bahraich, Bareilly, Badagu, Etah, Farrukhabad, Moradabad and Saharanpur districts. The productivity of groundnut can be increased substantially by adopting the following intensive methods.

Sowing at seed rate, sowing time and distance:

It is often seen that farmers use groundnut seed in small quantity, due to which the number of plants in the field is less and the yield is adversely affected. Therefore, it is necessary to use only the prescribed quantity of seeds for different species of groundnut. It would be appropriate to sow early maturing species in the second fortnight of July to avoid vad necrosis disease. Right time of sowing seeds

Use of Balanced Fertilizers:-

Application of fertilizers is very necessary for getting good yield of groundnut it's fair. If done Nitrogen 20 kg, Phosphorus 60 kg, Potash 45 kg (in element form), Gypsum 250 kg and Borax 4 kg per ha, should be used. If phosphate is used in the form of sigil super phosphate, then it is good if the prescribed amount of phosphorus is used in the form of sigil super phosphate, then there is no need to use gypsum separately. Complete quantity of nitrogen, phosphorus and potash fertilizers and half quantity of gypsum in the litter about 2-3 cm from the seed at the time of sowing by barber or robe. Must be deepened. Use the remaining half quantity of gypsum and the entire quantity of borax as a top dressing at the 3 week stage of the crop by spreading it and mix it well in the soil to a depth of 3-4 cm by light hoeing. Bacterial fertilizer which is known in the market as tree friend. Its quantity is 16 kg per ha. It would be good to apply as its use has been observed to increase the production of pods as well as ripening of pods in clustered species.

Seed Treatment

Before sowing, seed (kernel) should be purified with a mixture of thiram 2.0 gm and 1.0 gm Carbendazim 50% Ghuchu at the rate of 2 gm per kg seed or treated with thiophanate methyl at the rate of 1.5 gm per kg seed. Trichoderma 4 g + 1 g carboxin per kg. Seed treatment should be done. Treat the seed with specific Rhizobium culture of groundnut before sowing 5-6 hours after this treatment. One packet is enough for 10 kg of seeds. To mix the culture with the seed, dissolve 50 grams of jaggery in half a liter of water. Then mix 250 grams of Rhizobium culture whole packet in this solution, sprinkle this mixture over 10 kg of seed and mix it with light hand, so that a light layer is formed on the seed. Dry this seed in the shade for 2-3 hours and sow it till 10 in the morning or after 4 in the evening. There is a possibility of the culture bacteria dying in strong sunlight. In fields where groundnut is being sown for the first time or after a long time, the culture be sure to use.

Irrigation:-

If there is no rain and there is facility of irrigation, then two irrigations should be given at the time of pegging and pod formation.

Weeding:-

After 15 to 20 days of sowing, first weeding and after 30 to 35 days of sowing, second weeding must be done. While making pegging, weeding should not be done. Pendimethalin 30 EC for chemical weed control. of 3.3 L. / Hey. or Elaclor 50 EC. of 4 l / ha. Or 600 ml of Oxyfluorphane 23.5 EC. Quantity 700-800 liters. Spraying should be done after sowing by making a solution in water and before sowing i.e. 3-4 days after sowing. Due to this spray, there is no accumulation of seasonal grasses and broad-leaf weeds.

Excavation and Storage::-

It has been observed that farmers dig groundnut before the full maturity of the crop in order to get better price in the market and to speed up the sowing of wheat. Due to which the development of the grain is not good, the grain is of poor quality and the yield is reduced. Therefore, dig it only when the veins emerge on the skin of the peanut and the inner part becomes brown in color and the grain of the peanut becomes pink. After digging, dry the beans thoroughly and store them. If soaked peanuts are stored, the pods will become black in color, which becomes completely unsuitable for eating and seed.

Pests:-

White Guidar of Peanut:

Identification: Its girders eat the roots of plants and dry up the whole plant. The girders are yellowish-white in color, with a brownish-brown or reddish head, they turn round like a gondola when touched. Its mature peanut does not harm the crop. After the first rain, it copulates on the surrounding trees and again after 3-4 days it goes to the fields and lays eggs. If the adults are killed on the trees, the growth of their numbers will be greatly reduced.

The Treatment:-

  • Spraying of carbaryl 0.2% or monocotophos 0.05% or phanthoate 0.03% or chlorpyriphos 0.03% should be done within 2-3 days at the onset of monsoon to kill the adult pest on nutritive trees like neem, sycamore etc.
  • Chlorpyriphos 20 EC 3-4 hours before sowing. Or Qunalfas 25 EC. 25 ml per Treat the seed at the rate of kilogram seed and sow it.
  • In case of infestation in standing crop, 4 liters of chlorpyrifos or quinalphas chemical per hectare. Use with irrigation water at the rate.
  • Any sol pheromone should be used.

Termite:

Identification

They cut roots and pods in dry conditions. Plants dry up due to root cutting. The soil fills the place of the kernel inside the pod. Treatment of Termites can be prevented by using seed treatment and insecticide for white girder along with irrigation water.

Peanut Crown Rat:-

Identification

The groundnut being marked is affected by this disease. black spots on the affected area rises which is clearly visible.

Treatment

For this, seed treatment should be done.

Diroot Rat or Charcoal Rat:-

Identification

Due to lack of moisture and high temperature, this disease occurs in the roots. root turning brown and the plant dries up.

Treatment

Treat the seed and maintain moisture in the field. Adopt long crop rotation.

Bud Necrosis:-

Identification:

top buds dry up. The flood stops. In sick plants, new leaves become smaller and grow in clusters. Usually the plant remains green till the end, flowers and fruits are not formed.

Treatment:

Sowing should not be done before the fourth week of June. Control of thrips pest which is the carrier of the disease with the help of following insecticides Do it with medicine. Dimethoate 30 EC at the rate of one liter per hectare.

Groundnut Tikka Disease (Patradag) :-

Identification

Light brown round spots are formed on the leaves, surrounded by yellow circles on the lower surface. Due to severe infestation, spots are also formed on the stem and flower branches. Treatment: Zinc manganese carbament 2 kg on standing crop. Or Zeeneb 75 percent soluble powder 2.5 kg. Or 3 liters of jeerum 27 percent liquid or 2 kg of jeeram 80 percent. 2-3 sprays should be done at an interval of 10 days.
