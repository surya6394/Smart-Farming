Sugarcane Cultivating States in India:-

Sugarcane is a major cash crop in our country, which is cultivated in about 30 lakh hectares of sugarcane cultivated area every year. The average sugarcane yield in this country is 654 tonnes per hectare, which is much less than the production potential. Uttar Pradesh covers an area of ​​19.38 lakh hectare with an average yield of 58.7 tonnes per hectare. Most of the sugarcane cultivation in sub-tropical region is done in smooth alluvial lands which have sufficient water holding capacity. The favorable time for the growth of sugarcane lasts only from July to October. Sugarcane cultivation is generally done in sandy loamy land whose soil moisture is 12-15%.

Seed Sugarcane Selection and Quantity:-

Sunath the seeds from a pure disease and pest free field (nursery) with abundant manure and water. The upper 1/3rd part of sugarcane has relatively good deposition. According to the thickness of sugarcane, 50-60 quintals (about 37.5 thousand three-three eyes or 50.00 thousand two-two eyes paise per hectare) seed is required. For late sowing, one and a half times of the above seed is required.

Seed Treatment:-

Dissolve 280 grams or 580 grams or 580 grams of Baviston or 112 grams of Baviston per hectare in 112 liters of water and treat sugarcane trees by immersing the sugarcane trees.

Land Treatment:-

Gamma BHC for termite and pest borer control. 20% solution 625 l / ha or chlorpyrifos 20% solution 5.0 l. / ha by dissolving in 1875 liters of water or phorate 10 g or Sevidal 4: 4 rava 25 kg./ha. OR Reagent 0.3%, rava 20 kg/ha should be covered over the pads. For termite control, linden 1.3% dust or fonavalerate 04% dust should be covered by spraying 25 kg / ha on the trees.

Row to row distance:-

  • Autumn sowing 90 cm.
  • Spring sowing 90 cm.
  • Late sowing 60 cm.

Fertilizer Quantity:-

  • nitrogen - 150-180 kg/ha
  • phosphorus - 60-80 kg/ha
  • Potas - 20-40 kg/ha
  • zinc sulfate - 25 kg/ha

Usage Time:-

1/3 of the total amount of nitrogen fertilizer and 60 to 80 kg. Phosphorus and 20 40 kg, potash per hectare in element form. Before sowing, it should be put in the litter. The remaining two-third quantity of nitrogen should be used in two equal parts before June (between). Weeding should be done by giving zinc sulphate near the plants at the time of field preparation or after the first irrigation.

Irrigation:–

It has been found beneficial to do 45 irrigations in the eastern region of the state, 5-6 in the central region and 7-8 in the western region (after two irrigations).

Gulai:-

To provide moisture and air to the roots of sugarcane plants and from the point of view of weed control, it is beneficial to do one hoeing/shovel/cultivator after each irrigation in summer.

Earthing up:-

Light soil should be applied at the root of sugarcane plants at the end of June and lastly enough soil in July.

Sugarcane Greetings:-

The first greeting is about 150 cm high at the end of July, the second greeting is about 50 cm after the first greeting. Above in August, as required, three police stations of two lines should be congratulated simultaneously (Kanchi Badhai) in August-September.

Harvesting:-

November to April depending on age of crop, maturity, sugarcane species and time of sowing But should be harvested..

Prevention:-

  • After harvesting sugarcane, dry leaves should be spread in the field and burnt.
  • Under no circumstances should seeds be distributed from affected areas to unaffected areas.
  • As far as possible, trees of affected fields should not be taken.
  • After cutting the head of the heavily affected crop, it should be sent to the mill inside all the canes.
  • Before sowing in the affected areas, all the sugarcane pieces are clothed in 0.1% Malathion solution. It should be sown after soaking and rinsing.
