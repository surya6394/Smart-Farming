Mustard

Mustard has a prominent place in rabi oilseed crops. Despite many efforts in the state, there is no significant increase in the area. The main reason for this is the increase in the area of ​​important crops due to increase in irrigated capacity. Its cultivation is more profitable in the condition of limited irrigation. Adoption of advanced methods increases production and productivity.

Preparation of the field:-

The first plowing of the field should be done with a soil-reversing plow and after that, the field should be made dry. If there is less moisture in the field, then it should be prepared by pulwa. tractor drive Good preparation is done in one go by rotavator.

Alkaline / Saline land :-

For saline and saline areas for alkaline / saline land - Narendra Rai (NDR-8501), C.S. 52 and C.S. 54

Seed Rate: -

seed rate 5-6 kg/ha in irrigated and unirrigated areas rate should be used.

Sowing time and method:-

The suitable time for sowing rye is from the last week of September to the first fortnight of October. Sowing should be done at a distance of 45 cm in shallow (4-5 cm deep) litter behind the country plow. After sowing, a light pad should be used to cover the seed. In unirrigated condition, suitable time for sowing is second fortnight of September. If sowing is delayed, there is a greater chance of outbreak of mahu and other pests and diseases.

Quantity of Fertilizers:-

Fertilizers should be used on the basis of soil test recommendations, in all areas, 120 kg phosphate 60 kg and potash 60 kg per hectare yield good yield. Use of phosphorus in the form of single super phosphate is more beneficial. Because it also makes the availability of sulfur. If single super phosphate is not used, to ensure the availability of sulfur, use sulfur at the rate of 40 kg / ha and in unirrigated areas, half the amount of suitable fertilizers should be used as basal dressing. If D is used, then at the time of sowing it is beneficial to the crop at the rate of 200 kg gypsum per hectare and to get good yield, 60 quintals per hectare should be used rotten dung manure. In irrigated areas, half quantity of nitrogen and full quantity of phosphate and potash should be given 2-3 cm below the seed with barber or cloak at the time of sowing. Remaining quantity of nitrogen should be applied in topdressing after the first irrigation (after sowing).

Weeding and sparring:-

Within 15-20 days of sowing, it is absolutely necessary to remove the dense plants and make them 15 cm apart. To destroy weeds, one weeding should be done before irrigation and second after first irrigation. To control weeds by chemical, before sowing, 2.2 liters of Pasuchloralin 45 EC should be dissolved in 800-1000 liters of water per hectare and mixed well in the soil by running harrow or Pendimethalin 30 EC at the rate of 3.3 liters per hectare. Dissolve in 800-1000 liters of water and spray it evenly within two to three days of sowing.

Irrigation:-

Rye is particularly sensitive to lack of moisture during flowering and grain filling stages. Hence, irrigation should be given to get good yield. If fertilizer is applied in heavy quantity (120 kg nitrogen, 60 kg phosphate and 60 kg potash per hectare) and soil is light, then 2 irrigations respectively 30-35 days after first sowing and second rainfall to get maximum yield. If not, do it after 55-65 days.

Crop protection:-

The following are the pests and diseases on the rye crop:-

  • Alternaria leaf blight
  • White mite disease
  • Powdery mildew disease
  • Tulsita disease

Major action to be followed: –

  • In order to reduce the number of pathogens, deep plowing, crop rotation burning of diseased plant residues and destroying of weeds are very important during summer.
  • Early sowing is also helpful in preventing disease like Alternaria leaf scorch, white mite and powdery mildew etc.
  • Healthy and clean seeds should be used. 2.5 g Thiram per kg for protection against seed borne diseases. Seed or Metalaxyl 1.5 g/kg. By treating the seeds at the rate of seed, white mite and Tulasita disease are prevented in the initial stage.
  • 50 grams of Trichoderma powder per kg. Seed treatment at the rate of seed is also helpful in the management of various diseases. At the time of crop preparation 5.0 kg. Trichoderma based biofungicide 2.5 q. Mix it in the soil by investing in cow dung manure.
  • Certified seed of disease resistant/tolerant species should be sown. For example - Alternaria, T.4 for scorch disease, Y.R.T.T. - 6, and R. H.-30 etc.
  • For the prevention of white mite and Tulasita disease, 2.0 kg of Mancozeb 75 percent. Dosage or 1 kg of Metalaxyl + Mancozeb (Ridomil MZ). The drug should be mixed in 800- 1000 liters of water and sprayed. A prophylactic (inhibitor) spray one month after sowing is beneficial.
  • At the time of sowing in rye mustard, 60 kg. Potash per hectare should be applied as basal dressing before sowing. Phosphate should be used through single super phosphate, if it is available and if it is not available then DAP will supply phosphate. Through 60 kg. and 40 kg. Sulfur should be used at the rate of per hectare. 75 kg in rye-mustard. Nitrogen should be used as a top dressing after 30-35 days from sowing. Many diseases can be prevented by using balanced fertilizers.

Identification of scorch disease-

In this disease dark brown spots are formed on the leaves and pods in which round circular rings are clearly visible only on the leaves. Use any one of the following chemicals to treat them.

1 Macozeb 75 percent 2 kg per hectare
2 Geranium 80 percent 2 kg per hectare
3 Geneva 75 percent 2.5 kg per hectare 3.5
4 Cumin 27 percent liters per hectare
5 Copper Oxychloride 80 percent 3.0 kg per hectare

Identification of white ocher disease-

White blisters are formed on the lower surface of this disease, for the treatment of this, spraying Ridomil M.Z-72, mixed with 800-1000 liters of water at the rate of 2.5 kg/ha is beneficial.

Identification of Tulacita disease:-

In this disease, there is white hairy mildew on the lower surface of the leaves and yellowing on the upper surface, for its prevention, the use of chemicals controlled by white ocher.

Major pests of rye mustard:-

Mustard sawfly:

It is about 4-5 ml smaller in size than the black shiny colored house fly. There is a long fly. Due to the shape of the underopter of the female fly, it is called a saw fly. The trunks of this insect are black and gray in colour. These leaves can be found at the edges or of different sizes. It eats very fast making holes, in case of severe outbreak, the whole plant becomes leafless.

dentification of the painted insect:-

Adults are 5 to 8 mm long, bright black, orange and red colored rashes. The head is small triangular, the eyes are black and bulging. Its adults and young suck the juice by pricking their stinging and sucking mouthparts in the soft leaves, branches, stems, flowers and pods of the plants, due to which the infected leaves dry up from the edges and fall off. In infected beans, grains are less formed and oil content is also less in them. Its attack can happen anytime from October till the crop is harvested and goes to the barn.

Hairy caterpillar:-

It is a yellow or orange colored black-headed caterpillar. Its whole body is covered with thick black hairs. Its dungs ​​cause damage to the crop. Initially it causes damage by eating soft leaves of plants in herds and later in single form.

Cabbage Butterfly:-

The adult butterfly is yellowish white in color and the body surface is smoky white in colour. There is a black mark on its front wing. Its newborn nymphs initially eat the surface of the leaves in herds and later start eating the leaves from the edge and keep eating them on the inside. In case of severe outbreak, the whole plant is eaten.

Leaf tunneling insect (Knock insect) :-

The adult of this insect is a small black colored fly. Its female lays eggs by penetrating her incisors in the leaves, from which the newborn larva comes out and eats it by tunneling in the leaves, as a result of which irregular shaped white lines are formed in the leaves.
