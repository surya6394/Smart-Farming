Scientific Cultivation of Hybrid Paddy:-

Paddy is one of the three important food crops in the world and is the staple food of 2.7 billion people. It is cultivated in about 150 million hectares in the world and 135 million hectares in Asia. In India, about 44 million hectares and in Uttar Pradesh, about 50 million hectares, paddy is cultivated in different conditions irrigated, irrigated, waterlogged, unirrigated shallow and flood affected areas. There is a need to emphasize on the development of high yielding hybrid varieties of paddy for different conditions i.e. favorable irrigated and unfavorable conditions. The first hybrid species development program started in China in the year 1964. The hybrid species developed after the tireless efforts of the last 20 years is yielding 15-20% more production than the common species because of the available hybrids and effective root system, moderate level of resistance to drought and soil salinity. With hybrid species, the farmer can get more yield by successful diversification from limited resources in less area. At present, an area of ​​about one million ha is covered by hybrid species in India and so far a total of 35 hybrid species have been developed in the country. In which the species recommended for cultivation in Uttar Pradesh are Narendra Sankar Dhan-2, Pant Sankar Dhan-1, Pant Sankar Dhan-2, Ganga and Sahadri-4, Proagro 6201, Proagro 6444, PHB 71 and Pusa RH 10 (Fragrant). is major. It is known that hybrid varieties are developed by the combination / interbreeding / hybridization of male and female species with two different genetic properties, in which the seed of the first generation is used as a new variety because in the first generation a unique ability was found. The cultivars which are capable of giving higher yield than the best common varieties, keep in mind that in the next generation, due to the loss of their aggregate properties, there is a great decline in the energy efficiency and the yield is reduced. As a result, farmers have to buy hybrid seeds every year.

Land section:

Proper crop management is essential for full production potential utilization of hybrid paddy. Loam or clay land is suitable for getting a good crop of hybrid paddy. They have more water holding capacity.

Seed Rate::-

15-18 kg. The seed per hectare is sufficient, which is less than the seed rate of the common species is half.

Nursery Management:

The nursery management of hybrid rice is different from that of other high yielding common varieties. For planting hybrid paddy in one hectare area, nursery of 700 to 800 square meter area is sufficient, which is also desired for normal paddy. Keep in mind that even though the quantity of hybrid paddy seed is less for nursery, it is not appropriate to reduce the area. As a result, the plants in the nursery are rare and they grow well. 100 kg before nursery sowing. Nitrogen, 50 kg phosphorus and 50 kg. Potash is applied in the field at the rate of per hectare. Spraying of 0.5 percent zinc sulphate and 0.2 percent ferrous sulphate solution is desired if symptoms of zinc or iron deficiency are seen in the nursery.

Seed treatment::-

After soaking the pure seeds in water for 24 hours, carbendazim (Bavistin) at 2 g/kg. Treat the seed at the rate. The treated seeds should be spread on the pucca floor in the shade, covered with wet sack and straw and water should be maintained 2-3 times a day to maintain moisture. So that the seed germination can be done well.

Printing::-

Transplanting one to two plants with 2-3 buds of 25-30 days of age at 2-3 cm. Row to row at depth 15 cm. And 15 cm from plant to plant. It is advisable to do it at a distance. So that at least 45-50 capital per square meter must be there. The plants of the same hybrid species must be transplanted in place of the dead plants within a week of transplanting.

Fertilizer Management:-

To get good yield of hybrid paddy, 150 kg. Nitrogen, 75 kg, Phosphorus and 75 kg. Potash and 25 kg as required. Zinc Sulphate is required per hectare. Half of the nitrogen and the full amount of phosphorus and potash should be given at the time of and remaining quantity of nitrogen in two equal parts at the time of flowering and at the time of formation of gobha. As far as possible, the amount of fertilizer should be ensured only after getting the land tested and 10-15 tonnes of cow dung manure or green manure should be used.

irrigation:-

It is necessary to irrigate the field two days after transplanting. Keep in mind that it should not remain constant in the land and it is beneficial to maintain the water level in the field for 5 patients at the stage of filling the grain. it happens.

weed control::-

Within a week of transplanting, butachlor 3 ltr/ha or anilophas 1 ltr/ha by dissolving in 600-800 ltr of water and spraying or two weeds, 20-40 days after transplanting, weeds can be easily controlled.

Crop Protection::

Paddy's bunka, stem borer, paddy odoriferous and military insect are prominent. Insect chemicals should be used only at the time of severe infestation, as far as possible, methods of integrated pest management should be adopted. The spores are more prone to scorch and blight disease in hybrid species, only treated seeds should be used for their control.

Harvesting, Threshing and Yield:-

The water should be removed from the field after 20 days of 50 percent earing or when milk is formed in the lower kernels, when 80-85% grains become golden in color or after 30-35 days after earing, harvesting should be done. This can prevent the rash from falling off. Unwanted plants should be removed from the field before harvesting.

Income Expenditure Details:

With good management and use of suitable species, Rs. 3000/- to Rs. 5500/- per hectare. The benefit of the hybrid species can be increased and the farmer brother can produce the hybrid seed and earn Rs. 30000 to Rs. 50000 hectare can earn profit and 60 to 80 men get employment especially women farmers in the program for seed production.

Evaluation of seed purity:

For identification of pure seed, instead of traditional grow out test, molecular method can be used to identify pure seed in less time and at cheaper rates. One thousand samples can be easily evaluated in a day and only Rs. 6, whereas the traditional method not only takes more time but is also costly.

Precautions/Key Points:

In order to take full advantage of the genetic potential of hybrid rice varieties, its seed should be used every year because the seeds obtained from the hybrid paddy crop give relatively less yield in the second year and there is variation in height, maturity and grains in the second year crop. Whereas there is sufficient homogeneity in the first crop of hybrid paddy. Since for the best cultivation of hybrid paddy, only 15-18 kg. Seed / hay is used, so nursery management is absolutely necessary.
